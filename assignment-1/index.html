Why do i want to become a full-stack developer?

I want to become a full-stack developer because I'm passionate about building complete web applications, from the front-end user interface to the back-end server-side logic. I'm fascinated by the idea of creating a seamless user experience, where every aspect of the application, from the visual design to the data storage and retrieval, works together harmoniously.

As a full-stack developer, I'll have the opportunity to:

  • Work on diverse projects: Full-stack developers can work on a wide range of projects, from e-commerce websites to social media platforms, and everything in between.
  • Develop a broad skill set: To be a successful full-stack developer, I'll need to acquire a broad range of skills, including programming languages, frameworks, databases, and more.
  • Solve complex problems: Full-stack development involves solving complex problems that require a deep understanding of both front-end and back-end technologies.
  • Collaborate with others: As a full-stack developer, I'll work with designers, project managers, and other developers to bring projects to life.
  • Stay up-to-date with industry trends: The field of full-stack development is constantly evolving, and I'll need to stay current with the latest technologies and best practices.

Overall, becoming a full-stack developer will allow me to combine my passion for problem-solving, creativity, and technology to build innovative and effective web applications that make a real impact on people's lives.

My top 10 favorite books are:

  1. "Gods Are Not to Blame" is a play by Ola Rotimi, a Nigerian playwright. The play, published in 1971, is a retelling of Sophocles' ancient Greek tragedy "Oedipus Rex," set in the context of Yoruba culture and mythology.
  2. "Things Fall Apart" is a novel by Chinua Achebe, published in 1958. It's a classic of modern African literature and one of the most widely read and influential books of the 20th century.
  3. "Animal Farm" is a novella by George Orwell, published in 1945. It's an allegorical tale that critiques the Russian Revolution and the rise of Stalinism.
  4. "Half of a Yellow Sun" by Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie: A novel about the Biafran War and its effects on individuals and society.
  5. "The Lion and the Jewel" by Wole Soyinka: A play that explores the tensions between tradition and modernity in a Nigerian village.
  6. "The Joys of Motherhood" by Buchi Emecheta: A novel that explores the complexities of motherhood and womanhood in Nigerian society.
  7. "No Longer at Ease" by Chinua Achebe: A novel that explores the tensions between tradition and modernity in post-colonial Nigeria.
  8. "The Beautyful Ones Are Not Yet Born" by Ayi Kwei Armah: A novel that explores the corruption and decay of post-colonial Ghana, with implications for Nigeria and other African countries.
  9. "Stay with Me" by Ayọ̀bámi Adébáyọ̀: A novel that explores the complexities of marriage, family, and identity in contemporary Nigeria.
  10. "Children of Blood and Bone" by Tomi Adeyemi: A fantasy novel that draws on Nigerian mythology and culture, set in a world inspired by West African history and traditions.
